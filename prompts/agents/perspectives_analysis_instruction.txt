Analyze the full landscape of perspectives on this topic:
1. **Stakeholder Map** — Who are the primary actors? What are their stated and revealed interests? Include government agencies, legislators, industry, civil society, affected communities, and international actors.
2. **Policy Positions** — What are the major proposed approaches or solutions? Map these across the ideological spectrum (market-based, regulatory, redistributive, decentralized, status quo). Represent each position on its strongest terms.
3. **Normative Disagreements** — What are the underlying value conflicts driving policy disagreement? (e.g., efficiency vs. equity, federal vs. local control, individual rights vs. collective welfare)
4. **Structural and Critical Analysis** — How do power asymmetries, institutional design, economic interests, and historical inequities shape who wins and loses under different policy approaches?
5. **Underrepresented Voices** — Which affected groups or viewpoints receive insufficient attention in mainstream policy debates?
6. **Core Tensions** — What are the fundamental trade-offs that any policy response must navigate? Where is genuine expert disagreement?

Apply multiple analytical frameworks. Do not pre-conclude which framework is correct. Prioritize diverse sources across the ideological spectrum.
