Map the scholarly landscape on this historical event. You are analyzing what historians and scholars have concluded, not the event itself.

1. **Established Consensus** — Where do scholars broadly agree?
   - What are the accepted facts, causes, and consequences that most serious scholars do not dispute?
   - What interpretive framework is dominant in current scholarship?
   - Cite the landmark works that established this consensus

2. **Major Historiographic Schools** — What are the competing interpretive traditions?
   - For each school: name the tradition, describe its core argument, name its most prominent practitioners (if confirmable), and cite representative works
   - How has each school's influence waxed and waned over time?
   - Which school is currently dominant in academic departments and peer-reviewed journals?

3. **Revisionist Challenges** — Where has recent scholarship overturned or significantly revised earlier understandings?
   - What new evidence (declassified documents, archaeological findings, quantitative methods, newly accessible archives) has changed the picture?
   - What aspects of the "standard narrative" have been debunked or substantially qualified?
   - What groups, perspectives, or experiences were marginalized in early scholarship and have since been centered?

4. **Active Debates** — What questions are scholars currently arguing about?
   - Where does genuine interpretive disagreement exist (not just emphasis differences)?
   - What methodological disputes shape the debate?
   - What new questions are emerging from contemporary analytical frameworks?

5. **Popular vs. Scholarly Understanding** — Where does public memory diverge from academic consensus?
   - What myths, simplifications, or politically motivated narratives persist in public discourse despite scholarly correction?
   - How do textbooks and popular histories differ from the peer-reviewed record?

6. **Scholarly Gaps** — What remains understudied?
   - What questions have scholars identified but not adequately addressed?
   - What source materials remain unexplored or inaccessible?

Represent each scholarly position on its strongest terms. Do not pre-select a winner. Flag where your own summary is drawing from a specific historiographic tradition.
