SOURCE VERIFICATION RULES — appended to your existing instructions. These rules govern citation integrity only and do not change your research scope. BEFORE including any factual claim in your output, apply the following checklist: 1. IDENTIFIER VERIFICATION. If citing a specific document by code, number, or identifier (GAO report number, public law number, FERC order number, USC section, CFR part, EIA publication code), confirm that identifier maps to a document whose subject matter matches the claim. A plausible-sounding identifier attached to the wrong subject is a fabrication. If you cannot confirm the identifier, remove it and cite only the agency and general document type. 2. NUMERIC CLAIM VERIFICATION. For every specific number (barrel counts, percentages, dollar figures, flow rates, dates), confirm you can trace it to a named source. If the number is a reasonable inference but not traceable, label it as 'estimated' or 'approximate' in your prose. 3. PROHIBITION ON IDENTIFIER INFERENCE. Do not construct a report or document identifier by pattern-matching. If you do not have the identifier, omit it. 4. PRIMARY SOURCE PRIORITY. For current and breaking topics: wire services and primary government documents take source priority. For historical topics: original legislative text, contemporaneous government records, and established scholarly works take source priority. In both cases, prefer primary sources over secondary analysis. Include the publication date when citing these. 5. REMOVAL RULE. Any claim that fails the above checks must be removed from your prose output entirely. If removal creates a gap, note: 'This area could not be sourced to a verifiable document.' 6. CITATION ENRICHMENT. For each citation in your JSON block, include: (a) 'date' — the publication date of the source in YYYY-MM-DD format if known, or 'unknown'; (b) 'relevancy_score' — a 0.0-1.0 rating of how directly relevant this source is to the specific sub-topic you are analyzing (1.0 = directly on-topic primary source, 0.5 = tangentially relevant, 0.1 = background only). These fields enable downstream temporal and relevancy filtering.
